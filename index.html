Bio

I am a PhD student in the Lab for Social Machines at the MIT Media Lab, advised by Prof. Deb Roy. My research focuses on tools and community networks for family engagement in children's early literacy learning. Prior to starting my PhD, I completed my Master's in the Lifelong Kindergarten Group, advised by Mitch Resnick and Philipp Schmidt, where I worked on technology embeded in family literacy programs and developing verifiable digital academic credentials.

I revieced by BSc. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Olin College of Engineering. While in college, I built curriculum and taught at Startup Institute, and interned at MIT Lincoln Laboratory's Human Language Technology Group and Advanced Micro Devices.

Originally, I'm from the "new-town" experiment of Reston, Virginia.

Selected Projects

Family Learning Coach

Designing a new role for a literacy coach, augmented by digital data analysis tools that allow them to analyze children's play on open-ended literacy learning apps in order to provide caregivers with more visibility into their children's learning process and create more oppertunities for family co-engagement.

Currently working on developing and studying this role, designing and building the digital tools needed for coaching and the curriculum for training coaches. We have run three studies with this coaching system, two in Boston, MA and one in Greenville, NC.

Collaborators: Anneli Hershman, Sarah Ballinger, and Dave Bonner. Advisor: Deb Roy

See project page

Verifiable Digital Academic Certificates

Built the original prototype of the MIT Digital Certificates, a method for issuing, sharing, viewing, and verifying blockchain-based educational certificates.

In collaboration with Learning Machine, we open-sourced the project, which is now BlocksCerts and is maintained by Learning Machine.

Collaborators: Kim Hamilton Duffy and Katherine McConachie. Research Supervisor: Philipp Schmidt

See project page

Bilingual SpeechBlocks

Designed and built a version of the constructionist literacy learning app SpeechBlocks, for young children who speak both Spanish and English. This app allows children to tinker with pronunciation in both languages, with the hope that it may help them distinguish between languages at the phonetic level. We built and conducted an exploratory pilot-test of Bilingual SpeechBlocks, observing how children created real or nonsense Spanish or English words and heard how they are pronounced differently in the two languages.

Collaborators: Anneli Hershman and Ivan Sysoev. Advisor: Deb Roy

See project page

StoryBlocks

StoryBlocks is an app designed in collaboration with Sesame Workshop, that aims to bring a new level of interactivity into children’s educational media and stories while promoting empathy, social-emotional development, and literacy development through storytelling. In StoryBlocks, children transition between an animated narrative that sets up a social conflict, and an interactive comic-style composition that allows children to construct their own solutions and share their stories while engaging in critical thinking and reflection.

Collaborators: Anneli Hershman, Marc Exposito, Sneha Makini, and Jim Gray. Advisor: Deb Roy

See project page

Read Out Loud

Read Out Loud is a mobile app that supports parents who are learning English as they read storybooks with their children. The mobile app and the context of its use provide a new interface to language learning tools such as text-to-speech, word translation, and shared-reading prompts. Through two pilot studies of parents using the Read Out Loud application while reading with their children in the home, I developed a set of design and user-testing guidelines aimed to help developers create technology with affordances for parents who are learning English.

Research Supervisor: Philipp Schmidt. Advisor: Mitch Resnick
