Juliana Nazaré

I am a master’s student in the MIT Media Lab’s Lifelong Kindergarten Group and an alum of Olin College of Engineering, where I studied electrical and computer engineering. I worked as a Technical Associate at Startup Institute Boston and have interned at MIT Lincoln Laboratory in the Human Language Technology Group, Advanced Micro Devices (AMD), and Mozaic Design. My interest is in using computer science to build educational experiences that help people learn about themselves, their communities, and the world around them. Research Teaching Experience More...

At the Media Lab, my research focuses on building tools to explore how technology can help adult learners improve their basic literacy and numeracy skills and create new pathways to employment. Tools I have built include Read Out Loud, an application to help parents who are learning English improve their vocabulary and pronunciation while reading with their children, and the Media Lab Digital Certificates, a toolkit to create and issue immediately-verifiable digital certificates that convey a learner’s community membership rather than a checklist of their skills.

Lifelong Learning Workshop - Media Lab's India Initiative (Gandhinagar, India)
January, 2015
Co-mentored a week-long workshop for 40 undergraduate students. During the workshop, students were introduced to the basics of user-centered design through working on team projects to design and build tools for adult learners in India.

Mobile Device and Application Development - Olin College of Engineering (Needham, MA)
September - December 2014
Co-taught a student-run, four-credit course on mobile development. Course teaches Android, mobile design principles, rapid prototyping, and how to use collaboration tools such as Github. Worked with two other students to create the curriculum and run the class.

Introduction to Arduino Workshop - Google CSSI High School Camp (Cambridge, MA)
August 2014
Co-taught the Google CSSI High School workshop in Arduino programming at the Google office in Cambridge, MA. Worked with a five-person team to create the curriculum and run the class.

Introduction to Natural Language Processing - Startup Institute (Boston, MA)
April - May 2013
Created and ran an introductory natural language processing (NLP) workshop for 50 high school students where they learned the basics of computer science and NLP by programmatically processing their own Facebook feeds.

Introduction to Web Development - Startup Institute (Boston, MA)
October - May 2013
Created, organized, and taught a part-time program, RampUp, that helps business professionals with little CS background learn programming and web development basics. Was responsible for creating the original web development curriculum and running the program in Boston. The program is now running in Startup Institute’s Boston, New York, and Chicago offices.

Differential Equations - Olin College (Needham, MA)
September - December 2011
Teaching assistant for the course.
